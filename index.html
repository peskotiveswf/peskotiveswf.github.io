Biography

I’m an incoming Ph.D. student at MIT EECS, recently graduated from National Taiwan University (NTU) with a Bachelor’s degree in Electrical Engineering.

During my undergrad studies, I researched on speech and language understanding under Prof. Hung-Yi Lee and Prof. Lin-Shan Lee at NTU. I also worked on machine learning problems for computer vision such as multi-label learning and transfer learning under Prof. Yu-Chiang Frank Wang at Academia Sinica/NTU.

I will be joining the Spoken Language Systems Group at MIT CSAIL this fall, working with Dr. James Glass.

Multi-Label Zero-Shot Recognition with Knowledge Graphs

Inspired by the way humans utilize semantic knowledge between objects of interests, we propose a novel deep learning framework that incorporates knowledge graphs for describing the relationships between multiple labels to achieve multi-label zero-shot learning (ML-ZSL).

Sketch-based Image Retrieval across Art Styles

In this project, we try to perform cross-domain image retrieval across different art styles using sketches as queries. Given a sketch image and a target art domain, we aim to retrieve images that are semantically similar in that particular art domain.
